Billfold

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Billfold is built to work entirely offline, on your device. This policy is short because there isn't much to say — Billfold does not collect, transmit, store remotely, or share any of your information, because it has no server, no account system, and no network connection to send anything over.

What Billfold does with your data

Everything you enter into Billfold — bills, spending entries, savings goals, notes, settings — is saved only in your device's local storage. It never leaves your phone unless you personally choose to export a backup file and move it somewhere yourself (for example, to a cloud drive, email, or another device).

What we don't do

  • We don't collect any personal information.
  • We don't require an account, sign-up, or login.
  • We don't use analytics, crash reporting, or advertising SDKs.
  • We don't share, sell, or transmit your data to anyone, because we never receive it in the first place.
  • We don't use cookies or any web tracking technology.

Permissions Billfold uses

Billfold may request the following device permissions, used strictly for the features described:

  • Notifications — to remind you before a bill is due, if you turn reminders on. Scheduled entirely on your device.
  • Storage / file access — only when you choose to save or open a backup file yourself.

None of these permissions involve sending data anywhere. They exist only to let features you control run locally on your device.

Backups you create

If you export a backup file, that file is created and saved by your device's own operating system, wherever you choose to put it (Files, Google Drive, email, etc.). Billfold has no visibility into or control over what happens to that file once it leaves the app — that's entirely up to you and whatever service you save it to.

Children's privacy

Billfold does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children, since it does not collect information from anyone at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes — for example, if a future version of Billfold adds an optional cloud sync feature — this page will be updated first, and the change will be described clearly here before it applies.
